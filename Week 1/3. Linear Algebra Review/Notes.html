Matrix

A 2D array.

Dimension of matrix: number of rows x number of columns

Example:

4x2 matrix:

[ 1 2 3 4 5 6 ] \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \\ 5 & 6 \\ \end{bmatrix} ⎣⎡​135​246​⎦⎤​

2x3 matrix:

[ 1 2 3 4 5 6 ] \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} [14​25​36​]

Matrix Elements

A i j = A_{ij} = Aij​= to the i t h i^{th} ith row, j t h j^{th} jth column

Example:

[ 1402 191 1371 821 949 1437 147 1448 ] \begin{bmatrix} 1402 & 191 \\ 1371 & 821 \\ 949 & 1437 \\ 147 & 1448 \end{bmatrix} ⎣⎢⎢⎡​14021371949147​19182114371448​⎦⎥⎥⎤​

A 11 = 1402 A_{11} = 1402 A11​=1402

A 12 = 191 A_{12} = 191 A12​=191

A 32 = 1437 A_{32} = 1437 A32​=1437

A 41 = 1448 A_{41} = 1448 A41​=1448

Vector

Special case of a matrix where one of the dimensions is 1.

A n x 1 matrix

Example:

y = [ 460 232 315 178 ] y =\begin{bmatrix} 460 \\ 232 \\ 315 \\ 178 \end{bmatrix} y=⎣⎢⎢⎡​460232315178​⎦⎥⎥⎤​

Vector element

y i = i t h y_i = i^{th} yi​=ith element

y 1 = 460 y_1 = 460 y1​=460

y 2 = 232 y_2 = 232 y2​=232

y 3 = 315 y_3 = 315 y3​=315

y 4 = 179 y_4 = 179 y4​=179

Note that sometimes, indexes start at 0 rather than 1.

1-indexed vs 0-indexed.

Assume that 1 indexed is used.

Addition and Scalar Multiplication

Matrix addition

Can only add 2 matrix of same size.

[ a b c d ] + [ e f g h ] = [ a + e b + f c + g d + h ] \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} e & f \\ g & h \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a + e & b + f \\ c + g & d + h \end{bmatrix} [ac​bd​]+[eg​fh​]=[a+ec+g​b+fd+h​]

Example:

[ 1 0 2 5 3 1 ] + [ 4 0.5 2 5 0 1 ] = [ 5 0.5 4 10 3 2 ] \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 2 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 0.5 \\ 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 0.5 \\ 4 & 10 \\ 3 & 2 \end{bmatrix} ⎣⎡​123​051​⎦⎤​+⎣⎡​420​0.551​⎦⎤​=⎣⎡​543​0.5102​⎦⎤​

Scalar multiplication

[ a b c d ] ∗ x = [ x a x b x c x d ] \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} * x = \begin{bmatrix} xa & xb \\ xc & xd \end{bmatrix} [ac​bd​]∗x=[xaxc​xbxd​]

[ a b c d ] / x = [ a / x b / x c / x d / x ] \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} / x = \begin{bmatrix} a/x & b/x \\ c/x & d/x \end{bmatrix} [ac​bd​]/x=[a/xc/x​b/xd/x​]

Example:

3 ∗ [ 1 0 2 5 3 1 ] = [ 3 0 6 15 9 3 ] 3 * \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 2 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 \\ 6 & 15 \\ 9 & 3 \end{bmatrix} 3∗⎣⎡​123​051​⎦⎤​=⎣⎡​369​0153​⎦⎤​

Matrix Vector Multiplication

Number of columns in first matrix must match number of rows in second matrix.

A 3x2 matrix can be multiplied to a 2x1 matrix because the former has 2 columns and the later has 2 rows.

Start along the row of the first matrix and multiply by the value in the coresponding column of the second matrix and add up consecutive values.

[ a b c d e f ] [ g h ] = [ a ∗ g + b ∗ h c ∗ g + d ∗ h e ∗ g + f ∗ h ] \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \\ e & f \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} g \\ h \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a * g + b * h \\ c * g + d * h \\ e * g + f * h \end{bmatrix} ⎣⎡​ace​bdf​⎦⎤​[gh​]=⎣⎡​a∗g+b∗hc∗g+d∗he∗g+f∗h​⎦⎤​

[ 1 3 4 0 2 1 ] [ 1 5 ] = [ 16 4 7 ] \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 \\ 4 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 16 \\ 4 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} ⎣⎡​142​301​⎦⎤​[15​]=⎣⎡​1647​⎦⎤​

Matrix Matrix Multiplication

Like matrix vector multiplication, the first vector must have the same number of columns as the second matrix has rows.

Multipying a x * m matrix to a m * y matrix outputs a x * y matrix.

[ a b c d e f ] [ g h i j k l ] = [ a ∗ g + b ∗ i + c ∗ k a ∗ h + b ∗ j + c ∗ l d ∗ g + e ∗ i + f ∗ k d ∗ h + e ∗ j + f ∗ l ] \begin{bmatrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} g & h \\ i & j \\ k & l \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} a * g + b * i + c * k & a * h + b * j + c * l \\ d * g + e * i + f * k & d * h + e * j + f * l \end{bmatrix} [ad​be​cf​]⎣⎡​gik​hjl​⎦⎤​=[a∗g+b∗i+c∗kd∗g+e∗i+f∗k​a∗h+b∗j+c∗ld∗h+e∗j+f∗l​]

[ 1 3 2 4 0 1 ] [ 1 3 0 1 5 2 ] = [ 11 10 9 14 ] \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & 2 \\ 4 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 5 & 2 \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} 11 & 10 \\ 9 & 14 \end{bmatrix} [14​30​21​]⎣⎡​105​312​⎦⎤​=[119​1014​]

Matrix Inverse and Transpose

Matrix Inverse

What matrix do you have to multiply some other matrix by to get the indentity matrix?

This will be the result of the inverse of a matrix.

Note that m x m means it has to be a square matrix.

Only square matrices have an inverse

If A is a m x m matrix, and if it has an inverse,

A ( A − 1 ) = A − 1 A = I A(A^{-1}) = A^{-1}A = I A(A−1)=A−1A=I

Example:

[ 3 4 2 16 ] [ 0.4 − 0.1 − 0.05 0.075 ] = [ 1 0 0 1 ] \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 4 \\ 2 & 16 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0.4 & -0.1 \\ -0.05 & 0.075 \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} [32​416​][0.4−0.05​−0.10.075​]=[10​01​]

Matrix Transpose

Let A be an m x n matrix, and let B = A T B = A^T B=AT .

The B is and n x m matrix, and

B i j = A j i B_{ij} = A_{ji} Bij​=Aji​

Example:

A = [ 1 2 0 3 5 9 ] A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 3 & 5 & 9 \end{bmatrix} A=[13​25​09​]

A T = [ 1 3 2 5 0 9 ] A^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 \\ 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 9 \end{bmatrix} AT=⎣⎡​120​359​⎦⎤​
